Solving the Energy Problems one Block at a Time

Time for Transformation

Evolution of communication networks with the introduction of 5G and the ability to implement Self Organizing Networks designs. On the top of that the introduction of Smart Contracts with Blockchain Ethereum and IPFS. All of this is making it possible to being forward new Energy Business Models that will truly transform the domain.

Liberalisation of Energy Markets

Elasticity with private or public clouds. Transition of business models towards the “as a service “ paradigm. All these New services are emerging, which create both challenges and opportunities

Internet of Services

Enabling  complex business relationships between multiple stakeholders and creating Innovative business applications.

Integration Optimization and Decentralization

Integrate renewable energy generation production and distribution. Optimizing trade-offs between reliability, sustainability, and cost. And most importantly, decentralize the generation and distribution and cost models

The Design Elements

Not only the local interaction between the various technologies, but also the interaction with the overall energy system as a whole.

Connectivety
End-to-end connectivity between large varieties of grid elements, including distributed energy resources, building energy management systems and electric vehicles using public as well as private communication infrastructures.
Management
Smart Energy introduces a lot of new managed elements with increased data volume. Future Internet offers e.g. concepts for device registries, SW maintenance, Big Data analysis, network management, distributed processing.
Service Enablement
Future Internet enables new service platforms supporting e.g. multi-tenancy, dynamic pricing and billing services for instant collaboration between all relevant stakeholders including the prosumer.
Distributed Intelligence
Future Internet Technologies will introduce new technologies into hardware and – even more so – in software, effectively injecting intelligence into the grid, e.g. to coordinate and control Distributed Energy Resources.
Security and Privacy
Future Internet Technologies will provide new and improved means to support security and privacy

Electric Vehicles

The aim of this solution blueprint is to define a typical set of scenarios and functions for electric mobility, investigating the interests of a wide range of stakeholders and the likely evolution of their requirements in the coming years as market conditions evolve.

Demand Side Management
Controlling the load drawn by electric vehicles when they are charging to help maintain grid stability, while at the same time optimizing renewable energy usage. 
E-Roaming
E-Mobility roaming refers to the situation in which an EV User is using an EVSE within a contract with an E-Mobility
Provider that is not the EVSE Operator of the used EVSE 
Vehicle to Grid (V2G)
The idea is to look at EV batteries not only as electrical loads, but also utilize them as electrical sources whenever it is  affordable for the EV user, or beneficial to the connected smart building, micro grid or regional distribution grid.

Microgrids are smart, small-scale versions of future electricity systems and therefore will be an integral part of a future smart grid landscape. Microgrids will have a massive impact on the future electricity smart grid architecture and the associated control network.

E-MarketPlace

The need for an Electronic Market Place for energy arise from the evolution of the electricity market due to de-regulation processes, and the transformation of the old electricity grid into a smart grid. This grid will have to be able to cope with the integration of electricity from renewable sources – green but with a certain degree of unpredictability – variable loads when up to now loads are well known and fixed, and the integration of the end customer as an active element in the electricity grid.

Energy contract brokering
As supply and demand for electricity must be balanced at all times, it is important that there are mechanisms in place that adapt electricity generation to demand and
vice versa.
Incentive based green market
Spread the participation to the Green Market of the Energy
Prosumers providing different kind of incentives.
Trading flexible capacity
Some “prosumers” may have spare loads (negative = extra generation, positive = extra consumption) that
can be used by others to balance their energy needs. The marketplace is where the exchange happens.
Supplier side local trading
Taking a closer look to the role of the Supplier, the objective of this scenario is to balance the energy flow
among e-Islands, taking into consideration Generators at a regional level. When a deficit/surplus at a local
level (e.g. city, regional) occurs, the Supplier can operate on the market to trade energy within the
Marketplace either to cover the excess in demand or in production.
Flatten demand curve
This scenario describes how an ICT application may contact the customers via a market and effectively
manage Demand Side Management (DSM) signals to reduce the power consumption in customer homes.
